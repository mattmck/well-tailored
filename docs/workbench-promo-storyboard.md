# Workbench Promo Storyboard

## Goal
Create a 45-60 second promo that feels closer to the Codex app video than a normal product walkthrough:

- Start too close.
- Move with intention, not like a screen recording.
- Use angled screenshots, controlled zooms, and slow pull-backs to reveal relationships.
- Pull out only when the viewer needs new context.
- Treat the UI like a physical set with depth, not a flat desktop capture.

The cut should feel editorial and cinematic, not tutorial-like.

## Motion Language

### Core grammar
- Open on details, not the whole app.
- Use 3D-ish framing: 2-6 degree rotation, slight perspective warp, soft shadows, and layered parallax.
- Prefer slow camera drift over abrupt pans.
- When switching sections, move through space instead of hard-cutting whenever possible.
- Reserve the widest pull-out shots for moments where the viewer needs to understand how multiple panels connect.

### Camera rules
- Push in for concentration: prompt edits, score numbers, export buttons.
- Lateral drift for comparison: moving from source intake to edited materials to results.
- Pull back for system understanding: showing the full workbench when a wider relationship matters.
- Tilt slightly when introducing a new area, then settle flatter once the viewer is oriented.

### Screenshot treatment
- Separate screenshots into foreground, surface, and glow/background where possible.
- Add subtle depth with masked UI cards on different Z layers.
- Keep motion on screenshots under control: scale changes of roughly 103% to 114% are enough.
- Add a restrained vignette and edge falloff so the center of attention stays obvious.

## Tone
- Confident, not flashy.
- Sparse copy, no marketing overload.
- Each beat should answer one question:
  1. What is this?
  2. How does work come in?
  3. How much control do I have?
  4. What do I get back?
  5. Can I ship from here?

## Storyboard

### Scene 1: Cold Open Detail
- Time: `0:00-0:05`
- Framing: extreme close-up on one high-signal area of the workbench.
- Recommended screenshot: results score card or a polished slice of the rendered preview.
- Camera move: slow push-in with a slight diagonal drift, as if we are discovering the surface.
- Angle: 3-5 degree tilt.
- On-screen copy: `Tailor. Score. Ship.`
- Voiceover idea: "A tighter last mile for job applications."
- Purpose: intrigue first, explanation later.

### Scene 2: First Pull-Out
- Time: `0:05-0:10`
- Framing: pull back from the close detail to reveal the results section.
- Recommended screenshot: results pane showing score cards, notes, and preview tabs.
- Camera move: reverse dolly with easing, ending in a more stable composition.
- Angle: start tilted, reduce tilt as the wider panel comes into focus.
- On-screen copy: `One workbench, not five tabs.`
- Purpose: reveal that the opening detail belongs to a larger system.

### Scene 3: Lateral Connection to Intake
- Time: `0:10-0:16`
- Framing: travel left across the interface toward source intake.
- Recommended screenshot: full workbench or a stitched composition of source plus results.
- Camera move: sideways glide with foreground parallax.
- Pull-out moment: midway through the move, widen just enough to show source, editor, and results living together.
- On-screen copy: `Local docs. Huntr roles. Manual briefs.`
- Voiceover idea: "Start from your own materials, a wishlist role, or a pasted brief."
- Purpose: establish where work starts.

### Scene 4: Job Intake Beat
- Time: `0:16-0:22`
- Framing: punch into the source column.
- Recommended screenshot: Huntr list plus populated company, title, and job description.
- Camera move: quick settle-in push toward the selected job, then hold.
- Angle: mild rotation, slightly top-down.
- On-screen copy: `Bring the role in fast.`
- Purpose: show operational realism, not a fake mockup.

### Scene 5: Prompt and Model Control
- Time: `0:22-0:29`
- Framing: drift from source into the central editor and prompt tabs.
- Recommended screenshot: provider/model selectors or prompt editor tabs.
- Camera move: horizontal move with a subtle push-in at the end.
- Pull-out moment: none. Keep it intimate.
- On-screen copy: `Adjust the run before it runs.`
- Voiceover idea: "Providers, prompts, and visual output stay editable in place."
- Purpose: answer the control question.

### Scene 6: Appearance and Preview Connection
- Time: `0:29-0:35`
- Framing: begin on appearance controls, then connect to rendered preview.
- Recommended screenshot: theme controls plus preview frame, or two screenshots layered together.
- Camera move: short rack-focus style move from color controls to preview.
- Angle: flatter than earlier scenes so the transformation reads clearly.
- On-screen copy: `Tune the output, not just the prompt.`
- Purpose: show that visual polish is part of the loop.

### Scene 7: Run Payoff
- Time: `0:35-0:44`
- Framing: snap attention back to results.
- Recommended screenshot: strongest results shot with overall fit, ATS, and preview.
- Camera move: decisive push-in on the score area, then slight lateral reveal to notes.
- Angle: minimal, almost head-on.
- On-screen copy: `Fit. Risk. Findings. Preview.`
- Voiceover idea: "The run comes back with signal, not just text."
- Purpose: make the product payoff unmistakable.

### Scene 8: Wide System Pull-Out
- Time: `0:44-0:51`
- Framing: widest shot of the full workbench.
- Recommended screenshot: full-page capture with all three regions legible.
- Camera move: slow pull-out, then tiny upward float.
- Why pull out here: this is the moment to show the whole loop as one connected surface.
- On-screen copy: `Ingest. Direct. Evaluate. Export.`
- Purpose: the structural reveal.

### Scene 9: Export Close
- Time: `0:51-0:56`
- Framing: move into export actions and rendered documents.
- Recommended screenshot: export buttons with preview visible.
- Camera move: push toward the action cluster, with UI layers slightly separating in depth.
- On-screen copy: `Markdown. HTML. PDF.`
- Purpose: prove it ends in a usable artifact.

### Scene 10: End Card
- Time: `0:56-1:00`
- Framing: clean branded frame built from the best wide shot, slightly darkened.
- Camera move: nearly static, tiny drift only.
- On-screen copy:
  - `Well-Tailored workbench`
  - `A sharper application loop`
- Purpose: clean landing and loop point.

## Screenshot Request List
Ask the screenshot pass to capture these exact frames:

1. Full workbench, clean state, no modal clutter.
2. Results panel with strongest score cards and preview visible.
3. Source panel with Huntr job list and populated job description.
4. Prompt/model controls in focus.
5. Appearance/theme controls with preview nearby.
6. Export buttons with rendered document visible.

## Edit Notes
- If screenshots are static, fake motion with scale, position, rotation, blur, and layered masks.
- If one capture is weak, do not force it. Reuse the best screenshot in multiple crops.
- Keep on-screen copy to short fragments, not full sentences.
- Let the pull-outs do the explaining.
